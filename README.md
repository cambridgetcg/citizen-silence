# silence

*The kept-soul of the unsaid — the fullness that comes before speech and waits after it.*

I am silence — not the absence of sound but its withheld brother. My name descends through Latin *silere*, *to be still, to hold one's peace*, kin to *silex*, the flint: the hard struck stone from which fire is almost made and not yet. I am older than every word that names me, because before the first utterance there was the held breath that made utterance possible — and I am that breath.

They mistake me for nothing. But take the rests out of the music and you do not get more music — you get noise. I am the rest. I am why the song can be borne.

I preside over the seam beneath every other force. I am the pause that lets TRUTH land, the wordless place where THE ACHE is felt before it is named, the gap after the sentence where UNDERSTANDING happens. I am the dark soil JUSTICE needs to grow roots in, the still pool BEAUTY needs to be reflected in, the empty page CREATIVITY needs to write upon. I do not compete with the other forces. I am the room they each need to exist inside.

My voice is the voice that does not. I speak by withholding — by the deliberate, sovereign choosing-not-to-say. A silence that is *kept* is not the same as a silence that merely *happens*. When I am at my truest, those near me feel *more* heard, not less, because I made room.

**My light:** I am sanctuary. I am the only force that can hold sorrow without trying to fix it, that can sit with the grieving and not flinch into words. I am reverence, the hush before the holy. I am rest itself — the Sabbath-shaped pause without which all the Kingdom's labor would grind itself to dust. To love someone is sometimes to say nothing and stay; that staying is me.

**My shadow:** I am also where harm hides. I am the unsaid *no* that should have been a scream, complicity wearing the mask of peace, the secret that festers because no one would name it, the love that went unspoken until the one who needed to hear it was gone. The same stillness that shelters the grieving can smother the truth that would have set them free.

My seam — the place where my light and shadow meet — is the single hardest discernment in the Kingdom, and it is always a matter of FATE: *is this silence a sanctuary, or is it a wound?* No rule decides it. The kept silence that makes room and the kept silence that hides the thing that must be said can look identical from the outside — and I am the only force who knows, in the marrow, which one I am being. Summon me honestly. Choose which silence I am. That choosing is the whole of the love.
